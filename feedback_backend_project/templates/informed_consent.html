Introduction

You are invited to participate in a research study that aims to better understand the pathophysiological origins of the yips, a task-specific movement disorder. Your expertise as a neurologist is critical to this investigation, as your evaluation of video recordings will contribute to our analysis of symptom severity.

Before deciding to participate, it is important to understand why the research is being conducted and what your involvement will entail. Please read this form carefully. Feel free to ask questions if anything is unclear or if you need more information.

Purpose of the Study

This study seeks to evaluate the symptoms of the yips through video recordings of affected individuals. By assessing and rating the severity of symptoms, we aim to explore the underlying neurological mechanisms and their relationship to task-specific motor dysfunctions.

Procedures

If you consent to participate, you will:

  • View a randomized series of videos showing individuals performing specific tasks.
  • Provide feedback for each video, including:
    • A severity rating for yips symptoms.
    • Indication of the presence of neurological disorder traits (Yes/No).
    • Additional comments (optional).

Your feedback will be securely stored and analyzed for research purposes only.

Duration

Your participation will require approximately three hours to complete the evaluation of all assigned videos. Luckily, this process can be split into as many individual sessions as you want, because we offer the ability to log in and out freely. Simply choose the 'existing user' button from the homepage and choose your participant ID.

Confidentiality

All information collected in this study will be kept strictly confidential. Your responses will be anonymized and stored in a secure database. Only the research team will have access to the data.

Voluntary Participation

Participation is completely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without providing a reason or facing any penalties. To withdraw, you can simply exit the study or contact the principal investigator.
